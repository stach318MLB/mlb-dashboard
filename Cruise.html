The two directions

Drive → Bermuda (no airfare): Cape Liberty in Bayonne, NJ is ~7.5 hours from Cleveland. Royal Caribbean's Independence of the Seas docks ~2–3 days right in Bermuda — caves, pink-sand beaches, and St. George's history, with a big casino and Broadway-style shows onboard. Cheapest path, but no jungle. Fly → Western Caribbean (from Galveston, TX): this is the adventure powerhouse — Cozumel, Costa Maya, Roatán, and especially Belize, where one excursion combines cave-tubing, zipline, and jungle Mayan ruins. Adds airfare, but it's the best fit for "excursions first."

Option 1 — Bermuda, 5 nights DRIVE

Royal Caribbean Independence of the Seas · round-trip Cape Liberty (Bayonne, NJ)

Drive: ~7.5 hr Cleveland → Bayonne, NJ — no flights, your own car at the port

Sample route: Bayonne → 2 full days docked at Royal Naval Dockyard, Bermuda → Bayonne

Excursions: Crystal & Fantasy Caves cave, Horseshoe Bay snorkel, St. George's UNESCO town history, glass-bottom boat

Kids' program: Adventure Ocean (3–12) — fits all three (5, 6, 9)

Casino & shows: Casino Royale + Broadway-style entertainment

Horseshoe Bay, Bermuda · Photo: Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA

Why it fits:

  • No airfare — easiest on the budget by far
  • 2 full days IN Bermuda = lots of excursion time, no private-island day
  • Caves + history tick two of Nicole's three wishes
  • Short enough for a first family cruise

Trade-off: one destination, and no jungle (that's the Caribbean's department).

Option 3 — Western Caribbean, 5 nights FLY

Carnival from Galveston, TX (fly CLE→Houston) · Cozumel + Costa Maya

Getting there: fly CLE → Houston, ~1 hr transfer to Galveston

Sample route: Galveston → Cozumel → Costa Maya → sea days → Galveston

Excursions: Mayan ruins at Tulum/San Gervasio & Chacchoben history, jungle ziplines & cenote swims near Costa Maya jungle

Recommended ages: ruins & cenotes work for all; some ziplines need age 5–8+ (the 5-year-old may sit a couple out)

Casino & shows: yes + live entertainment

Why it fits:

  • Real adventure excursions (ruins + jungle) without a full week
  • Two Mexican ports, no private-island day
  • Carnival = value pricing, keeps total down despite airfare

Trade-off: only two ports; misses Belize's signature cave-tube + zipline combo (that's the 7-night).

Option 4 — Western Caribbean, 7 nights FLY

Carnival from Galveston, TX · Costa Maya + Roatán + Belize + Cozumel

Getting there: fly CLE → Houston, ~1 hr transfer to Galveston

Sample route: Galveston → Costa Maya → Roatán → Belize → Cozumel → Galveston (4 ports, zero private-island days)

Belize is the star: one excursion = cave-tubing an underground river cave + zipline over the rainforest jungle + a Mayan ruin history

Plus: Roatán jungle ziplines & suspension bridges; Costa Maya & Cozumel ruins

Recommended ages: ruins/snorkel suit all; cave-tubing usually OK at 5 with a family operator; a few ziplines require age 6–8+, so the 5-year-old may swap to an easier option on those

Carnival ship in Cozumel, Mexico · Photo: Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA

  • Hits all three wishes — jungle, cave, history — in real ports
  • 4 excursion days, the most "adventure per trip" of any option
  • Casino, live entertainment, WiFi all included
  • Still comfortably under your $20k ceiling
