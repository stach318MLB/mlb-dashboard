2 · Bermuda DRIVE

5–7 nights from Cape Liberty (Bayonne, NJ) · ~7.5 hr drive · 2–3 days docked in Bermuda

5 · Mediterranean FLY

7 nights from Barcelona/Rome · Marseille · Genoa/Florence · Naples · Civitavecchia (Rome)

The history splurge — and the cheapest way to cruise Europe is MSC out of Barcelona (interior fares start ~$565/pp). It's also the priciest of the five once you add transatlantic airfare, so consider it the "dream/reference" option. Stops put you at the Colosseum, Pompeii, and Florence history — no jungle/caves, but unmatched ruins. A 2-night Rome or Barcelona stay is built into the estimate (your "cool city we haven't explored" idea).

What I'd do with this

If excursions truly come first and budget is secondary, Western Caribbean (Carnival, ~$13.7k) is the standout — Belize alone checks jungle, cave and history. Bermuda (~$8.9k, no airfare) is the easy, cheap, low-stress pick. Mexican Riviera (~$12.7k) is the best non-Caribbean adventure. The Mediterranean is the dream trip if you'll stretch toward the ceiling.
